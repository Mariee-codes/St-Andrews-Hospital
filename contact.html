Directions

St Andrews Hospital is conveniently located in the heart of Healthcare City, easily accessible by public transportation and with ample parking facilities for visitors and patients.

By Car

From Downtown: Take Main Street north, turn right onto Medical Center Drive. The hospital will be on your left.

Public Transit

Bus routes 15, 22, and 37 stop directly in front of the hospital. The nearest subway station is Health Center Station (Blue Line).

Frequently Asked Questions

What are your visiting hours?

General visiting hours are from 10:00 AM to 8:00 PM daily. Specialized departments such as ICU, Pediatrics, and Maternity may have different visiting policies. Please check with the specific department or call our information desk at (123) 456-7892 for more details.

How do I schedule an appointment?

You can schedule an appointment by:

  • Calling our appointment line at (123) 456-7891
  • Filling out the appointment request form on this page
  • Emailing us at appointments@standrewshospital.com
  • Visiting our reception desk in person during business hours

What insurance plans do you accept?

We accept most major insurance plans, including but not limited to BlueCross BlueShield, Aetna, Cigna, UnitedHealthcare, Medicare, and Medicaid. For specific questions about your insurance coverage, please contact our billing department at (123) 456-7893 or email billing@standrewshospital.com.

How can I access my medical records?

You can access your medical records through our patient portal at portal.standrewshospital.com. If you haven't registered for the portal yet, please bring a valid ID to our Medical Records department during business hours, or call (123) 456-7894 for assistance. You can also submit a request in writing to records@standrewshospital.com.

What should I bring to my first appointment?

For your first appointment, please bring:

  • Valid photo ID
  • Insurance card
  • List of current medications
  • Medical history information
  • Referral from your primary care physician (if required)
  • Any relevant medical records or test results
  • Payment method for copays or deductibles
